What Users Lose (Zoom V → Teams X)

These 2 features exist in Zoom but have no equivalent in Teams:

Content Sharing
Simultaneous Multi-Presenter Sharing
Zoom allows up to 4 people to share content at once. Teams supports only 1 sharer at a time. Impacts design reviews, code comparisons, and collaborative sessions.
Recording
Local Recording
Zoom allows recording directly to the local disk. Teams records to cloud (OneDrive/SharePoint) only. Users who relied on local files will need to download from the cloud.

What Users Gain (Zoom X → Teams V)

This feature is new for users moving to Teams:

Chat & Async
Persistent Post-Meeting Chat
In Zoom, meeting chat disappears after the session. In Teams, the chat thread persists — participants can continue the conversation, share files, and reference history after the meeting ends.

Contact Center — 8 features

Join & Scheduling

Events
Event Branding (Registration/Email)

Where Teams is Stronger (Partial vs V)

These 5 features work better in Teams than in Zoom:

Content Sharing
PowerPoint Live
Teams offers a native presenter mode with speaker notes, independent slide navigation for attendees, and real-time translation. Zoom only supports screen-sharing slides.
Layouts
Together Mode / Immersive View
Teams offers Together Mode (shared virtual space for up to 49) and 3D immersive events. Zoom's immersive view is more limited.
Layouts
Focus on Content Mode
Teams automatically hides participant video when content is shared, keeping focus on the material. Zoom's focus mode UX differs.
Accessibility
CART Captions Support
Teams has dedicated CART captions support for events. Zoom supports captions/interpretation but the method differs.
Security
Presenter Policy Controls
Teams has explicit "who can present" policies configurable by admins. Zoom relies on host-level controls during the meeting.

AI Features — Both Platforms Partial

AI is a licensing decision, not a platform decision
All 10 AI features (meeting summaries, action items, catch-me-up, voicemail transcription, chat summarization, call summaries, agent assist) are marked Partial/Partial on both platforms. Zoom requires AI Companion; Teams requires M365 Copilot. Neither platform includes full AI capabilities in the base license. The user experience will depend entirely on which AI add-on the organization purchases.

Contact Center — Needs Separate Migration Plan

8 out of 8 Contact Center features: Zoom V vs Teams Partial
Zoom has a native, fully integrated Contact Center product. Teams does not — it relies on 3rd-party solutions (e.g., NICE, Genesys, Landis) connected via Azure Communication Services. If the organization currently uses Zoom Contact Center, migrating this workload to Teams is a separate, complex project that requires evaluating 3rd-party vendors, integration architecture, and agent retraining. It should not be bundled into the general meetings/rooms/phone migration timeline.

Key Messages for End Users

  1. "Most of what you know still works" — 44% of features are identical on both platforms. The core experience of joining meetings, sharing content, chatting, and making calls carries over.
  2. "Screen sharing changes" — Only 1 person can share at a time in Teams. Plan ahead for sessions that need side-by-side comparisons — use PowerPoint Live or pre-arranged layouts instead.
  3. "Recordings go to the cloud" — No more local recordings. Files will be in OneDrive/SharePoint — accessible from anywhere, but requires downloading if you need an offline copy.
  4. "Your meeting chat lives on" — Unlike Zoom, Teams keeps the meeting chat thread alive after the meeting. You can continue conversations, share follow-ups, and find files there.
  5. "AI is similar — licensing is different" — If the org licenses M365 Copilot, you get meeting summaries, action items, and catch-up features. Without it, these features will be limited — just like Zoom without AI Companion.
  6. "Contact Center users — stay tuned" — The CC migration is a separate workstream. Your current workflows will be communicated separately with dedicated training.
